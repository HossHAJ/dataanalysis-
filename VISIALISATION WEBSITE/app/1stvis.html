Repartition of vehicles by brand and engine type

Graph Interpretation

This graph represents the cbr code of the different car brands. Each Part of the pie chart represents a specific car brand, with its size indicating the number of cars for every brand present in the dataset. Finally it's interactive, if you click on a brand you will see all the different cbr code with their proportion in the brand. We can see on this graph that the brand with the most car is MERCEDES and VOLKSWAGEN.

1. Diesel GO :

- Highly polluting altough diesel is oxides of nitrogen (NOx) and fine particulate emissions. - Emits around 2.68 kg of CO₂ per liter combusted.

2. Gasoline ES:

- Having fewer fine particulates than diesel but are higher in CO₂ per liter. - Approximately 2.34 kg CO₂ per liter combusted.

3. Ethanol/bio-ethanol E85/ EH:

- Depends on the source of the biofuel, agriculture (deforestation, fertilization) could result in a imprint. - Approximately 1.55 kg CO₂ per liter, depending on sourcing for biofuel.

4. LPG petroleum or gas / GP/ES gasoline:

- LPG gas is a liquefaction of petroleum gas, therefore it is a good fuel with an impact of CO₂ emissions , without particulates and low NOx. - Lack of particulates and approx 1.65 kg CO₂ per liter combusted.

Initially and then Rank from Most to Least Polluting:

  • 1. Diesel (GO) → High NOx emissions wich is nitrogen oxides.
  • 2. Gasoline (ES) → More CO₂ than Diesel, but exhausting fewer air pollutants.
  • 3. LPG/Gasoline (GP/ES) → Few of air pollutants and somewhat less CO₂ combusted.
  • 4. Ethanol (EH) → Best Carbon footprint

To conclude on this chart, we can see that if we click on Lexus, we will find a major part of EH.
