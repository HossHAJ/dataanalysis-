Evolution of the ecological norms

Graph Interpretation

This graph presents an analysis of CO2 emissions (g/km) across different car brands. Each bubble represents a specific car model, with its size indicating the level of CO2 emissions.

- Horizontal axis: Represents different car brands.
- Vertical axis: Indicates CO2 emissions in grams per kilometer.
- Bubble size: The larger the bubble, the higher the CO2 emissions for that car model.

This visualization allows for a quick comparison of the environmental impact of various car brands and highlights the most polluting models. By hovering over a bubble, you can see more details about the corresponding car model.

In this graph, we can notice that the most polluting car is the Ferrari, This is normal because Ferrari offers cars with a V8 or V12 that have a big consumption of gas. On another hand, we have the Fiat 500 that is the less polluting car of the graph. It's because the Fiat 500 is a small car that was created to drive in a city, that's why it doesn't have a powerful motor, and a very low consumption.
